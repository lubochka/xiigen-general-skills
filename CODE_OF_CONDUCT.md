# Code of Conduct

XIIGen General Skills community spaces should be respectful, useful, and safe
for contributors, maintainers, users, and issue reporters.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and professional.
- Stay on topic and assume good faith where reasonable.
- Provide clear, accurate information when reporting issues or proposing
  changes.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, and contributor ownership rights.
- Report security issues privately according to `SECURITY.md`.
- Avoid posting secrets, private prompts, customer data, regulated data, or
  confidential source code.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, abuse, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Spam, impersonation, scams, vote manipulation, or repeated off-topic
  disruption.
- Publishing secrets, credentials, private keys, customer data, confidential
  source code, regulated data, or vulnerability exploit details.
- Encouraging malware, credential theft, unauthorized access, or unsafe use of
  AI-generated code.
- Misrepresenting a fork, package, service, or account as officially approved
  by XIIGen.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, remove, lock, or reject content that violates this
Code of Conduct or creates legal, security, privacy, operational, or community
risk. Maintainers may warn, restrict, or block participants when needed.

If sensitive information is accidentally posted, maintainers may remove or
redact it and may ask affected parties to rotate exposed credentials or follow
their incident response process.
